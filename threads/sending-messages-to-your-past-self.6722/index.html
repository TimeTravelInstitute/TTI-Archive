I think of our universe (timeline, worldline, etc...) as a piece of paper in a case of paper (the multiverse). All other worldlines are already there with things happening similarly, somewhat differently and radically differently from what we are experiencing here. If you could hop to the next closest sheet of paper, things would be very similar but not completely the same. Depending on what you did there, you could change things enough to reverberate out from that sheet to the ones around it. Information alone is all that is needed. Send a message to July 3rd on the next worldline over and it could cause ripples in the stack of paper, metaphorically. If you got an email today from someone claiming to be you from January, 2013 - would you take it seriously? If not, end of story, no ripples.

In my case, I sent an encrypted email to myself that contained three years worth of significant information. The initial ripples were staggering. I continue to experience these ripples in the timeline today. They have been occurring more frequently just recently. Since this version of "me" isn't actively manipulating the timeline, there is only one scenario I can think of that is causing these "altervus" - another "me" who is capable of changing the timeline. I gave my younger self everything he would need to start developing time/inter-dimensional travel. He's had fourteen years now. I think he's getting close.
