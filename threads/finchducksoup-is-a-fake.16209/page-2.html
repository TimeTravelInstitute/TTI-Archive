I do believe that Titor was "real" in the limited sense: there is a real human being behind the moniker.

Is he a real time traveler is the appropriate question. Absolutely not.

The chance the he, or anyone else for that matter, is a time traveler who travels against the arrow of thermodynamics (backwards) is basically zero. Unless and until I see a rigorous theory of physics that proposes backwards time travel I'll remain unconvinced. So far there is no such theory.

The best that can be imagined is based on a few (highly questionable) interpretations of GTR where the time coordinate has to be assumed to be negative based on an "imaginary" square root of "-1"...and where one has to dismiss the inertial mass limitations of "c" or "c+" velocities and which ignores "c" itself as a limiting factor. Pretty tenuous on both counts.

Yes, I see a lot of New Age theories that posit God-only-knows-what process allows time travel. Those theories cannot be disproven because they are based on faith.
