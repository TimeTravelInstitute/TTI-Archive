Debating the existence of God or god, or gods, of course has its parallels in science. There is a possibility that other space-time universes apart from this one exist in some kind of multiverse, isn't there? Most reasonable scientists and theorists will not discredit the possibility simply because no way has been devised to experimentally prove their existence. And in light of that possibility, it seems equally possible (to me anyway) that there might as well be some kind of omniscient assemblage of pure energy or consciousness or... something... floating around out there amongst the disparate universes. It's all conjecture isn't it? Everything outside of our ability to experiment and evidence may as well not exist; or may as well exist, for that matter.

I'll lay my cards on the table: I don't believe in god. I'm a deeply spiritual person, but god simply does not fit into my lexicon of the divine. I believe the Universe itself may have some kind of baseline consciousness associated with it, of which our minds are a sort of evolved convolution. That conscious may be, for all intents and purposes, like a god. But it wouldn't be something apart from the universe, some supreme governing mind, and it certainly wouldn't judge us or damn our souls on the basis of our sexual proclivities. Anyway, that's where I stand.

But in regards to this ongoing debate: it seems highly unlikely that either any side is going to make an inch of progress convincing any other. Atheism is as much a matter of faith as religion. It is of course more likely to be based in empiricism than belief in god is, though there are plenty of people on the holy side of the fence who would argue that their belief is based in the evidence of their senses.

In my opinion, this is the most reasonable position on the matter: one can not take the evidence of their senses, one way or the other, as proof in either direction. Our senses are fallible. While people who believe in god based upon their experiences might be misinterpreting some materialistic event as divine, people who have never seen any evidence of god may simply be ignoring or failing to interpret some very simple, clear signs. Since god is beyond empirical verification, all debate on the subject is moot. The wheel goes round and round and round. Wheee.

An eternity yes, or an infinite amount of space, which science tells us is most likely in the case of the real universe. Given an infinite amount of space, even with the constraints of finite time and finite ingredients to work with, all logically possible iterations of the given ingredients must exist, including human life (and perhaps including beings like God as well).

And why us? Why here? Why a planet at exactly the right distance from its sun? This, my friend, we call the Weak Anthropic Principle. i.e., things must have been that way because, lo and behold, we are indeed here in the position to ask "where did it all come from?" We are here because we must be here, because an infinite universe absent of us would be inconceivable.
