I don't think faster-than-light speed is necessary for time travel. It seems like killing a fly with a sledge hammer. And we probably could view other times with less energy than it would require to transmit matter. However, I'm not sure it would be technically easier aside from that. Regarding whether we could change the past or not, I think of it as painting a picture. Only the artist really knows all the strokes that go into the final image. A passerby only sees the finished work. With time travel, non time travelers will only see the net sum of all choices and actions made, whereas a time traveler could, I think, redo things as long as they wanted.

Also, I hope you don't mind me commenting. I clicked on your name to check your profile, which led me to your youtube channel. I watched the police chase video and thought it was fake at first, until I read a comment saying it was real. Then I looked at google and wikipedia and other sources and confirmed it. I was inspired by the story, and I hope all is working well for you. You see, no matter how things had been (guilty or innocent), I have had my own bad experience with law enforcement, though nothing so dramatic or long-lasting. If I had a time machine, I'd love to help out a person such as yourself even if only in small ways. Maybe someone else feels the same.

I cannot comment on the photo, since the zoom feature is not working. Though it could have been anyone, a small dose of paranoia can do wonders for one's self-preservation. It can add caution that helps to avoid getting caught.

Time is our most precious resource, and it should never be taken for frivolous reasons.

I don't think that the guy in the photo is beyond reasonable doubt a time traveller. We went to follow him but he was on a bicycle and he had long gone by the time we reached the bridge.

Yes we were very paranoid, you have no idea, police, press etc. But it didn't make us hide away in a hole any more than we already were, we were in Holland and outside of UK jurisdiction. At the time we had to consider him a police officer, even though he didn't look or behave like one, and we've both had years of experience of them. But his behaviour was so odd and out of place that it stuck in my mind even all these years later.

Something else happened last year, again with a photograph. I found a photo of me on the internet, or someone who looks a hell of a lot like me, that was taken in 1997. I'm guessing 97 because of my hair, clothes and the mobile I'm holding in the photo. It was tagged as Fiona Mont on flicker and was taken by a guy who says he didn't know who the woman in the picture was. He just took it at random while out walking in Hove.
