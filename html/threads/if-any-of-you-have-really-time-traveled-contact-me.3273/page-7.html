But seriously... sometimes I think it IS productive to ask people to answer engineering questions about their contraptions...like what some claimant thinks these contraptions will accomplish. The productive thing is to always ask yourself questions about how any contraption works, such that you can eventually build a mathematical model of it.

Building a system model is extremely productive. In fact, building a full-up 6-DOF model of a new airplane design is a complex, but productive activity that allows us to continually study modifications and improvements to the airplane system. I can spend up to TWO YEARS of my engineering time JUST building accurate dynamical system models of the airplane we wish to produce. It becomes an extemely important tool to answer questions and refine a working design into a highly efficient design.

So building a predictive system model in the digital modeling domain (i.e. MATLAB/Simulink) is one way to actually DO "trial and error testing" without even having to build the physical system yet. That saves LOTS of TIME and MONEY in the long run of making a revolutionary machine.
