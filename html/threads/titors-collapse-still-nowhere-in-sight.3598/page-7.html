It's a matter of a frame of reference. Sure, from the frame of reference of a time traveler s/he would be making factual statements about history rather than making predictions.

On the other hand, if the people to whom he delivers the message exist in the past relative to the events spoken of then the TT is making predictions about their future.

Pre dicta, peri dicta, post dicta : speak before, speak during, speak after. The TT is speaking of events, from their perspective, before they occur - prediction.

You're not wrong. It's just a matter of looking at it from both perspectives.

Of course "dicta" in legal terms means "editorial" opinions expressed by the Judge that have no bearing on the issues being heard in the case. That definition fits the Titor Saga.
