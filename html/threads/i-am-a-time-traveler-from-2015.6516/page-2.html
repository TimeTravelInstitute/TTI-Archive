Let's just assume for a moment that (whatever it is) could teach you to predict the lotto numbers. "You" is everyone. Who would bother the play the lotto if that was the case? If it is a dead bang assured probability that you could pre-determine the numbers then the lotto would, at first, be paid out in pennies - not dollars - because thousands of folks would correctly predict the winning numbers. After that the lotto collapses becayse the payout would be at best 1:1.

So, what value is there in a systen based on random chance and miniscule probability of winning where the outsome is assured not to be random and a commonly available value?

The same applies to the stock market. Let's use time travel to make a killing in the market. Let's go to Black Friday, 29-OCT-1929 and long the market for future winners and short the market for current losers. Great. We make a killing. But do we? Time travel is based on an infinitely "long" arrow of time. Today we might think that we are the only time traveler when we go back to Black Friday. Unfortunately, in front of us is "forever". An infinite number of time travelers (at least theoretically) get the same idea no matter where they are in time and they all go back and place the same or similar buys and short sells in the market (plus whatever other market strategies were available in 1929). It's an all buyers market on the long side and no sellers available on the short side (the long buyers bought out all available shares). Everyone still loses. The downside is time travelers would be responsible for an even deeper worldwide depression. WWII would probably start in 1930 rather than 1937 (in China). An early Spanish Civil War would be the WWII spark instead of China in the east or Poland in the West. And without any real market USA Lend-Lease to GB and the USSR would not occur. Do you like sauerkraut?

Using time travel to arbitrage the market will not work. Market movement is absolutely based on uncertainty of outcome.

An aside: Roosevelt's "Fair Deal" did not end the Great Depression. It extended it. The Great Depression was put on hold by the pre-USA entry into WWII combat by the Lend-Lease Program. US industry with, yes, an eye for profit, financed and equipped the Brits and Soviet Union from 1940 to 1944. The Soviets might have built nice Yak-3 fighter-bombers but they won the war in the east with Curtis P-40's and Bell P-39's - Made in the USA and exported under Lend-Lease. The two most common fighter aircraft in WWII (and the two aircraft with the most air-to-air kills). Hoo-rah!
