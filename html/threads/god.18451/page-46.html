Since god is beyond empirical verification, all debate on the subject is moot. The wheel goes round and round and round. Wheee.

An eternity yes, or an infinite amount of space, which science tells us is most likely in the case of the real universe. Given an infinite amount of space, even with the constraints of finite time and finite ingredients to work with, all logically possible iterations of the given ingredients must exist, including human life (and perhaps including beings like God as well).

And why us? Why here? Why a planet at exactly the right distance from its sun? This, my friend, we call the Weak Anthropic Principle. i.e., things must have been that way because, lo and behold, we are indeed here in the position to ask "where did it all come from?" We are here because we must be here, because an infinite universe absent of us would be inconceivable.
