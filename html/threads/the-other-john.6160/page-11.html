The whole 'cannot return home' theme arises from a fundamental misunderstanding of the science. It is in fact yet another way that one can KNOW that Titor was a hoax......as one can state for a fact that any scientists clever enough to create a time machine would also be aware of the Copernican way of thinkikng.

Copernican logic dictates that there is nothing 'special' about our place in the universe....or our timeline, if such things exist. If we travel to another timeline...there is not some marker left behind that says 'this is your home'. From the perspective of the timeline you have travelled to, your 'home' timeline is just another timeline in a sea of billions of them.

There's absolutely nothing preventing you returning 'home'......any more than there is something in your new timeline preventing some other time traveller from travelling to where you came from. The entire 'cannot return home' malarkey is derived from fictional stories, and is such a common element that it does not surprise me that it finds its way into hoaxes. But it has NO basis in science.....and it's clear that Titor was either unaware of that.....or just plain conived to make a good excuse for conveniently disappearing before the questioning got to hot.

So...Pamela...there's no reason whatever why Titor could not have made it home. Similarly, if he had a genuine time machine, there's absolutely no reason why he could not have shown up again any time between his departure and now. Don't forget that a time traveller is not bound to the same linearity and progression of time that we are. He could arrive home and then a minute later set the device to 2010. 10 years may have passed for us......but only 1 minute for him.

A time traveller effectively has all the time in the world. The notion that Titor just 'had to dash' back to 2038 to be with wife and kids and earn a living is nonsense.
